# Research task: Spotify developer policy + branding compliance

**Owner:** unassigned. Block Phase 3 of setlist-intelligence
(`showbook-specs/setlist-intelligence/phases/phase-03-spotify-export.md`)
from shipping until this brief is closed.

**Why this exists:** see SI-19 in
[`plan-review.md`](./plan-review.md). Phase 3 creates playlists on
the user's Spotify with custom cover art and "Made by Showbook"
branding in the description. Spotify's developer policy has
specific requirements about how third-party apps brand the
artifacts they create. Apps that ignore the rules can have their
developer account suspended — which would kick every Showbook
user's Spotify connection offline simultaneously. Nobody has read
the current policy.

---

## Prompt for a future Claude (or human researcher)

> You're doing a focused compliance research pass on Spotify's
> Developer Policy + branding guidelines for an app called
> Showbook. Showbook is a personal entertainment tracker
> (concerts, theatre, comedy, festivals). Phase 3 of its
> setlist-intelligence feature creates Spotify playlists on the
> user's account, with two flavors:
>
> 1. **Hype playlist** — pre-show. Named `Hype: {artist} —
>    {date} @ {venue}`. Contents are a predicted setlist for
>    tonight's show, resolved to Spotify track URIs.
> 2. **"What I heard" playlist** — post-show. Named `Live:
>    {artist} — {date} @ {venue}`. Contents are the actual
>    setlist the user attended, in performance order.
>
> Both playlists are **private** by default. The user can
> change visibility in Spotify itself. Both playlists carry a
> branded **cover image** generated by Showbook (a dark
> editorial card with `HYPE` or `LIVE` label, headliner,
> date, venue), uploaded via `PUT /playlists/{id}/images`. The
> playlist **description** says `Made with Showbook` or
> similar — the exact text is what you're researching.
>
> A separate yearly feature creates `Showbook · YYYY` playlists
> (one signature track per attended show, DJ-set-ordered) with
> the same branding pattern.
>
> **What to read:**
>
> 1. Spotify Developer Policy:
>    <https://developer.spotify.com/policy>
> 2. Spotify Design Guidelines (branding section in particular):
>    <https://developer.spotify.com/documentation/design>
> 3. The Spotify Developer Terms of Service:
>    <https://developer.spotify.com/terms>
> 4. Anything else linked from those that's relevant to apps
>    that **create playlists** or **modify users' libraries** —
>    pay attention to the playlist creation, cover-image
>    upload, and metadata sections.
>
> **What to produce — a short markdown report at the bottom of
> this file** with these sections:
>
> 1. **Branding requirements for Showbook-created playlists.**
>    - Mandatory wording in the description? (e.g., "Powered by
>      Spotify"? Disclaimer that we're an independent app?)
>    - Logo placement constraints in cover art? (Can we
>      include the Spotify logo? Are we required to?)
>    - Maximum allowed size/positioning of our own logo?
>    - Anything else.
>
> 2. **Naming conventions for the playlists.**
>    - Are we allowed to use "Spotify" in playlist names? In
>      descriptions? Anywhere?
>    - Any restrictions on emojis, special characters, etc.?
>
> 3. **Cover-image upload constraints.**
>    - File format / dimensions / size constraints (we know
>      ≤256 KB JPEG from the API docs — confirm).
>    - Any content restrictions on the image itself? (No
>      logos of other streaming services, etc.?)
>
> 4. **What we MUST NOT do.**
>    - Any patterns that get apps suspended? (Things like
>      auto-creating playlists without explicit user consent,
>      mass-creating, etc. — the user-consent rules are
>      especially important since our flow is one-tap.)
>    - Specifically: does Spotify require an explicit
>      user-facing consent prompt *each time* we create a
>      playlist, or is the initial OAuth grant enough?
>
> 5. **Anything else surprising.** (Rate limits with policy
>    teeth, audit-trail requirements, anything that affects
>    how we model the feature.)
>
> Keep the report tight — a paragraph per section is fine. The
> consumer is an engineer about to write the Phase 3 code
> who needs concrete "do this, don't do that" rules. Don't
> paraphrase the legal text; extract what we need to do
> differently from the current spec.
>
> Finally, if you find a hard blocker (e.g., Spotify forbids
> our use case entirely), flag it loudly at the top of the
> report. The phase plan needs to know.
